Understanding Material Roles in Flexible Packaging
Structural/Print Layer (Outer)

Stiffness, printability, surface protection

Gas, moisture, and light protection

Heat sealing, product contact

Each material serves specific functions in laminate structures, balancing quality requirements with cost efficiency.

Single Material Properties and Applications
Material Name Code Density (g/cm³) Key Single Property & Role Typical Products Packaged
Polyethylene Terephthalate PET 1.365 High Stiffness & Heat Resistance. Excellent print surface, good clarity, and high dimensional stability under heat (used as the primary outer print layer). Microwave Meals, Snack Trays, High-Speed Printed Films (e.g., confectionery wrappers).
Biaxially Oriented Polypropylene BOPP 0.905 High Clarity & Yield. Cost-effective, high-gloss film with good moisture barrier properties and low density (yields more film per kg). Pasta, Biscuits, Snack Foods, Cigarette Overwraps.
Low-Density Polyethylene LDPE 0.925 Excellent Heat Seal & Flexibility. Lowest melting point film; provides high dart impact strength (puncture resistance) and superior weld integrity. Always the Sealant Layer. Frozen Foods, Bread Bags, Produce, Heavy-Duty Shipping Sacks.
High-Density Polyethylene HDPE 0.955 High Rigidity & Toughness. Provides better stiffness and higher-temperature resistance than LDPE; useful for heavy or bulky products. Rice/Grain Bags, Detergent Pouches, Heavy-Duty Industrial Liners.
Nylon Film (BOPA) NYLON 1.145 Outstanding Puncture Resistance & Aroma Barrier. Excellent toughness, wear, and abrasion resistance, especially at freezing temperatures. Frozen Seafood, Cheese, Deli Meats, High-abuse applications (e.g., sharp-edged items).
Cast Polypropylene CPP 0.91 Strong Seal & Hot Tack. Used as a sealant layer, offering better thermal resistance (higher melting point) and faster sealing speeds than LDPE. Retort Pouches, Stand-up Pouches, Medical/Sterilization Packaging.
Polypropylene PP 0.908 General Rigidity. Used as a basic structural film where high clarity is not paramount; good balance of strength and cost. Bulk Grains, Textile Packaging, Non-food bulk goods.
Metallized and Specialty Material Properties

These materials gain their key properties through modification (metallization) or finish (matt/pearlized).

Material Name Code Density (g/cm³) Key Property and Role Typical Products Packaged
Metallized PET MPET 1.365 High Barrier Curtain (Light & O₂). Excellent barrier against light, oxygen, and moisture, achieved by depositing a thin layer of aluminium. Used as the barrier layer in high-specification laminates. Ground Coffee, Dried Herbs, High-end Nutritional Supplements, Lamination Barrier Curtains.
Metallized BOPP MBOPP 0.905 Cost-Effective Moisture Barrier. Provides a good moisture and light barrier, but at a lower cost and density than MPET. Common barrier layer in snack packaging. Potato Chips, Snack Mixes, Cereal Liners, Moisture-Sensitive Powder Mixes.
Matt Finish BOPP BOPP_matt 0.855 Tactile & Low-Glare Aesthetic. The surface is treated to give a low-gloss, paper-like feel. Primarily an outer layer used for marketing and shelf appeal. Gourmet Pet Food, Organic Snacks, High-end Candy Bars.
Pearlized BOPP BOPP_pearlized 0.79 High Opacity & Low Density. The film contains micro-voids, making it opaque and giving it the lowest density, thus maximizing yield (more meters per kg). Ice Cream Wraps, Candy Wrappers, Opaque outer layers requiring maximum coverage.
Twist PET PET_twist 1.335 Twist Retention. Designed to hold a twisted shape after wrapping (high "dead fold" property). Hard Candies, Taffy, Chocolate Bars (where the twist is part of the seal).
Colored HDPE/LDPE HDPE/LDPE_colored 0.96 / 0.925 Opaque & UV Protection. Pigment provides opacity, often eliminating the need for a metallized layer when light barrier is the main concern (and O₂ is secondary). Gardening Soil, Fertilizer Bags, Films requiring full light blockage.
Laminated Structures & Case Studies
Material Selection Balance

Flexible packaging material selection is a balancing act between achieving the necessary Quality Control (QC) properties (barrier, strength) and minimizing cost to ensure Efficiency and profitability.

Fundamental Material Selection by Role
Material Density (g/cm³) Key Role and Property
PET (Polyethylene Terephthalate) 1.365 Outer Strength. Excellent stiffness, high clarity, dimensional stability, and high chemical resistance.
BOPP (Biaxially Oriented Polypropylene) 0.905 Structural/Print. Good barrier, high gloss, low density, and cost-effective surface layer.
LDPE / HDPE 0.925 / 0.955 Sealant. Low melting point, high clarity, and high dart impact strength (puncture resistance). Always the innermost layer.
NYLON 1.145 Abuse Resistance. Excellent puncture and abrasion resistance (often used for frozen or abrasive products).
Metallized Films (MPET, MBOPP) ~0.905-1.365 Barrier Curtain. Provides an opaque, high barrier "curtain" against light, oxygen, and moisture.
Case Studies: Product Applications
Product 1: Ground Coffee Bag (High Barrier, Puncture Resistance)

Coffee requires an extremely high barrier to protect volatile aromatics and prevent oxidation.

Required Properties Why the Material is Chosen Alternative Layering
Puncture & Abuse Resistance NYLON (1.145) is often chosen as a sub-surface layer due to its superior toughness and abrasion resistance. Replace NYLON with thicker PET (1.365) for stiffness and clarity, if puncture resistance is secondary to print quality.
Oxygen & Light Barrier Metallized PET (MPET, 1.365) is the chosen "curtain" layer providing opaque barrier against Oxygen Transmission Rate (OTR). Use Aluminium Foil for zero OTR, or PET/SiOx for transparent, high-barrier film.
Seal Integrity LDPE (0.925) provides robust, low-temperature seal critical for maintaining pressure and freshness. Use Colored LDPE (0.925) for colored seals or CPP (0.91) for better hot tack performance.
Primary Lamination Choice: PET (1.365) / MPET (1.365) / LLDPE (0.925)
Product 2: Salty Snack Food (Moisture Barrier, Aesthetics, Cost)

Snacks require a high moisture barrier to maintain crispness, protection from light (to prevent oil rancidity), and an easy, clean tear.

Required Properties Why the Material is Chosen Alternative Layering
Moisture Barrier & Aesthetics Matt Finish BOPP (0.855) or Pearlized BOPP (0.79) provides moisture barrier with marketing appeal. Use Twist PET (1.335) for high-end, crinkly appearance often used for candies.
Light/Fat Barrier Metallized BOPP (MBOPP, 0.905) serves as cost-effective "curtain" blocking light and boosting moisture barrier. Use Colored HDPE (0.96) for opaque barrier without metallization cost.
Easy Tear & Seal CPP (Cast Polypropylene, 0.91) offers higher melting point for fast machinery and clean tear properties. Use BOPP/BOPP mono-material structure for easy-recycling with special sealant grade.
Primary Lamination Choice: BOPP (0.905) / MPET (1.365) / CPP (0.91)
Why Use Laminates? (The "Curtain" Concept)

The act of lamination (combining materials) is necessary because no single plastic film offers all four required properties (strength, printability, high barrier, and heat sealability).

Lamination Property Material Layer Role Example Rationale
Barrier Enhancement Metallized Film (MPET/MBOPP) The metal acts as an extreme "curtain" against O₂ and water vapor. MPET's inherent stiffness is maintained, but the metal provides the function that the plain polymer lacks.
Mechanical Strength Outer PET or NYLON Lamination allows the strength layer (PET) to protect the underlying barrier layer (MPET), preventing flex cracks or pinholes that compromise the curtain.
Chemical Resistance Adhesive in Lamination Lamination bonds the material layers, protecting the inks and ensuring that the final heat-seal layer (PE) never touches the print layer, avoiding ink smudge or chemical degradation.
Weldability Inner LDPE/CPP The low-cost sealant layer (PE) provides the required welding properties. It is laminated to the higher-cost barrier layers, ensuring the final bag is structurally sound and sealable.
Key Takeaways
Material Selection Principles
  • Match material properties to product protection requirements
  • Balance quality needs with cost efficiency
  • Consider processing requirements (sealing, printing)
  • Evaluate end-use conditions (storage, transportation)
Lamination Benefits
  • Combines multiple properties in one structure
  • Enables high-barrier protection
  • Provides mechanical strength and durability
  • Allows optimal material usage and cost control
Professional Insight

Successful flexible packaging design requires understanding both material science and practical manufacturing constraints. The right material combination ensures product protection, consumer appeal, and production efficiency.
